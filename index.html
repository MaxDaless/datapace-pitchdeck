The $100 Billion Problem You Don't See

Database is the #1 bottleneck for application performance (AppDynamics, 2024) and cloud costs (FinOps Foundation, 2023), yet the leading tools highlight issues, but rarely solve them.

This isn't a theory. Our Proof-of-Concept for PostgreSQL already proves we can uncover critical, costly inefficiencies that platforms like Datadog completely miss.

We are building the system of record for database performance, starting with a developer-first tool that pays for itself.

A Perfect Storm: Three Irreversible Trends

This pain is intensifying daily due to three massive market shifts.

Data & Complexity Tsunami

Explosion of data volume (181 zettabytes by 2025, Statista) and the rise of complex, multi-database stacks (Postgres, Snowflake, Mongo, etc.) make optimization impossible with old tools.

The Experience Economy

"Slow is the new down." Performance is no longer a feature; it's a direct driver of revenue and brand reputation. A mere 0.1-second improvement in site speed can boost e-commerce conversions by 8.4% (Deloitte, Google). Moreover, nearly half of all mobile visitors abandon pages that take over 3 seconds to load (Google).

The FinOps Imperative

With 32% of cloud spend being wasted (CloudZero, Flexera 2022/2023 reports), CFOs and CTOs are demanding tools that provide a clear ROI by improving efficiency. 51% of organizations now have a FinOps team, with another 20% planning one by 2025, highlighting the C-suite's growing focus on cloud cost optimization (State of the Cloud Report 2024).

Our Unique Moat: Navigating the Performance Landscape

The market is fractured into three types of players who only solve part of the problem.

Broad Observability

Datadog / New Relic

"Mile-wide" platforms that show you symptoms (e.g., high CPU), but lack the deep, prescriptive fixes developers need.

Data Observability

Sifflet / Monte Carlo

Focus on data quality and pipeline integrity, not the real-time operational performance of transactional databases.

DBA-Centric Tools

pganalyze / Redgate

"Mile-deep" tools that provide granular metrics but are built for expert DBAs, not the everyday developer.

Datapace is the missing layer. We combine a "mile-deep" focus with a developer-first approach to deliver prescriptive, AI-driven fixes that democratize DBA-level expertise.
